From Concept to Clinical Impact

Current pharmaceutical toxicity screening relies on metabolic viability assays like MTT and resazurin. While these provide quantitative measures, they report on downstream consequences of stress — metabolic impairment and membrane integrity loss — instead of upstream stress-signalling events.

CondenSense addresses this gap using liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) as a sensitive, early reporter of cellular stress. When cells experience oxidative damage, DNA stress, or proteotoxic insult, specific intrinsically disordered proteins rapidly condense into fluorescent puncta — providing a fast, visually unmistakable signal.

Why It Matters

The Unmet Need in Drug Safety

Current toxicity assays miss critical early warning signs, leading to costly late-stage drug failures.

The Detection Gap

Cells under significant biological stress, mounting active stress responses, and accumulating molecular damage may still return near-normal metabolic readouts. This creates a dangerous blind spot in early drug development where sub-lethal toxicity goes undetected.

Clinical Consequences

Drugs that pass standard toxicity screens can still fail in Phase II/III trials due to unexpected organ toxicity. The FDA estimates that toxicity accounts for approximately 30% of late-stage drug failures, predominantly hepatotoxicity and cardiotoxicity.

Economic Impact

Late-stage drug failures cost pharmaceutical companies billions. Detecting toxicity earlier in the development pipeline could save enormous resources and, more importantly, prevent harmful drugs from reaching clinical trials or the market.

The Problem

A New Approach to an Old Problem

Moving from death-based to stress-based toxicity sensing represents a conceptual advance in drug safety science.

Current Methods

Downstream Detection

Traditional assays like MTT, resazurin, and LDH measure consequences of damage that has already occurred.

  • Reports metabolic impairment after damage
  • Misses early stress-signalling phase
  • LDH and Annexin V are even further downstream
  • Insensitive to sub-lethal stress
CondenSense

Upstream Detection

Our biosensor detects the earliest cellular stress responses, before irreversible damage occurs.

  • Detects stress granule formation in minutes
  • Reports on upstream stress-signalling events
  • Graded and reversible response
  • Catches sub-lethal toxicity early
Our Technology

Modular Cassette Architecture

Three independently swappable modules enable customization for different stress pathways while maintaining the same detection platform.

Stress Promoter Cassette

Transcriptional sensors that respond to defined cellular stress axes. Examples include SoxS (oxidative), recA (DNA damage), and in mammalian cells, NRF2/ARE and HSP70 pathways.

IDR-Fluorophore Fusion

Intrinsically disordered regions (FUS 1-163 or G3BP1) fused to sfGFP or mScarlet-I enable real-time fluorescence microscopy without fixation.

Scaffold & Terminator

Optimized expression control elements including RBS, Kozak sequences, and poly-A signals. BioBrick RFC10-compatible for registry submission.
